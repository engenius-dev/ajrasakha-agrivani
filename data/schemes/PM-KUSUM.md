# Scheme Name: Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyaan (PM-KUSUM)
**State/Central:** Central Government Scheme (Implemented jointly by the Central Government and State Implementing Agencies (SIAs)).

**Target Audience:** Individual farmers, rural landowners, groups of farmers, cooperatives, panchayats, Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs), and Water User Associations (WUAs). Priority is given to small and marginal farmers and those using micro-irrigation.

## 1. Objective
To provide energy and water security to farmers, promote solar energy in agriculture, and de-dieselize the farm sector. It aims to reduce environmental pollution and enhance farmers’ income by allowing them to generate and sell surplus solar power. The scheme targets adding a solar capacity of 34,800 MW with a total Central Financial support of ₹34,422 crore.

## 2. Eligibility Criteria
* **General:** Must be an Indian farmer or beneficiary. No specific age limit is mentioned.
* **Component A (Solar Power Plants):** Farmers can set up Renewable Energy based Power Plants (REPP) of 500 kW to 2 MW on their barren or cultivable land. If they cannot arrange the equity required, they can opt for development through a developer or the local DISCOM.
* **Component B (Standalone Pumps):** Farmers in off-grid areas can install standalone solar agriculture pumps up to 7.5 HP (up to 15 HP for the North-Eastern Region, Hilly regions, and Islands).
* **Component C (Grid-Connected Pumps):** Farmers already connected to the grid can solarize their individual pumps (Solar PV capacity up to two times the pump capacity in kW is allowed) or participate in Feeder Level Solarization (FLS). Mixed feeders can also be solarized.

## 3. Exclusions (Who CANNOT apply)
* Non-farmers or entities without land access.
* Projects and equipment not meeting MNRE technical guidelines or lacking BIS certification.
* Delayed or non-compliant installations (liable to lose benefits/subsidy).
* New standalone solar agriculture pumps (Component B) are not allowed in Dark zones/black zones.
* In dark zones/black zones, only existing grid-connected pumps are eligible to be solarized.

## 4. Financial Benefits
* **General Support:** Subsidy support via Central Financial Assistance (CFA) provided by the Government. Farmers can avail of this Central CFA to install solar pumps even if states are not in a position to provide their state share.
* **Loan Assistance:** Bank finance is available, allowing farmers to initially pay only 10% of the cost, with the remainder covered by loans (up to 30%). For FLS feeder separation, loans can be taken from NABARD, PFC/REC, or RDSS.
* **Component A Income:** Farmers can earn a mutually agreed lease rent for providing their land. Alternatively, the generated solar power is purchased by DISCOMs at a feed-in-tariff (FiT) determined by the respective State Electricity Regulatory Commission (SERC).
* **Component B & C Subsidy (General States):** The Central Government provides 30% CFA, the State Government provides at least 30% subsidy, and the farmer provides the remaining 40%. For FLS, the CFA is up to ₹1.05 Cr/MW.
* **Component B & C Subsidy (NER/Hilly/Islands):** The Central Government provides 50% CFA, the State Government provides at least 30% subsidy, and the farmer provides the remaining 20%. For FLS, the CFA is up to ₹1.75 Cr/MW.
* **Surplus Power & Savings:** Under Component C (IPS), farmers can earn extra income by selling surplus daytime solar power back to the DISCOMs. Under Component C (FLS), farmers get day-time reliable power for irrigation free of cost or at a tariff fixed by the state.

## 5. Required Documents
* Aadhaar / Identity proof.
* Land ownership or usage proof.
* Bank account details and online portal registration details for subsidy transfer.
* Project/application documents submitted through the implementing agency.
* Executed Land Lease Agreement (if leasing land to a developer under Component A).
* Documentary evidence of connected load or GPS survey details (for Component C).
